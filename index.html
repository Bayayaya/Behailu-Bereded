Doctoral Student | Water Resources Engineering (Civil & Environmental Engineering)
Villanova University • Pennsylvania, USA

My research focuses on sediment-informed design of Green Stormwater Infrastructure (GSI) to improve long-term performance and resilience under dynamic storm conditions. I study how sediment is transported, captured, and deposited within stormwater systems—spanning roadway grate inlets, sediment forebays, and facility-scale processes. This work integrates controlled laboratory flume experiments, two-dimensional hydrodynamic and sediment transport modeling (SRH-2D, HEC-RAS 2D), and physics-guided machine learning to quantify capture efficiency, deposition patterns, and downstream impacts that influence maintenance and design decisions.

Research Areas & Applications

  • Sediment-informed GSI design and maintenance: Quantifies how sediment loading and particle size influence clogging, hydraulic capacity loss, and long-term performance of green stormwater infrastructure—supporting more resilient design and maintenance planning.
  • Experimental + physics-based modeling: Combines controlled flume experiments with 2D hydrodynamic and sediment transport models (e.g., SRH-2D, HEC-RAS 2D) to evaluate sediment capture efficiency, resuspension risk, and deposition patterns under varying storm conditions.
  • Physics-guided machine learning for prediction: Develops data-driven surrogates using SRH-2D outputs (e.g., depth, velocity, shear stress, bed change) to predict fine-sediment deposition maps and evaluate hypothetical GSI geometries for improved performance.
